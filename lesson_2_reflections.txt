1) What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?
To turn your ordinary directory, that keep only files, into a repository, that will keep the history of all files in that directory.

2) How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository? What value do you think it offers?
Staging area lists the files that will be in the next commit. Sometimes, not all changes on the working directory is part of the same logical change.
So, you can split your changes in one or more commits.

3) How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change?
Stage files that changes reflect the same logical change. Then commit.

4) What are some situations when branches would be helpful in keeping your history organized? How would branches help?
Branch will be helpfull when you want to develop something new that will need more then one commit to be achived.
